Getting started

September 24, 2018

I'm a beginner, I need an education. I'm not looking to create huge classical works, just some melodic minimal electro using synths.

I've enrolled in two coursera courses:

  • Music theory
  • Creating sounds for electronic music

I need some hardware to make sounds and an education to get me started. I've got a mac laptop and PC running both windows 10 and OSX (hackintosh).

What else do I need? A cheap midi controller.

I am totally sold on the idea of Roli midi controllers. These sexy pieces of kit are MPE enabled hardware, that really float my boat in terms of musical expression. The idea of creating realistic sounding string and wind instruments is very appealing. Not cheap though.

Next up, a DAW or "Digital Audio Workstation". This lets me record and manipulate/create sounds. I won't be hitting the road anytime soon with a live act but I would like to create music as close to realtime as possible. Sampling, cutting, looping. After looking at Ableton's session view, I'm super keen on finding a DAW with a similar view of the composition, alongside a traditional timeline. The DAW must support MPE with little to no setup headaches - so I can get the most out of my Roli.

I found two that seem promising:

  • Ableton live, no MPE support, but great live editing capabilities.
  • Bitwig, excellent MPE support. I hear that ex Ableton engineers started Bitwig. I've downloaded the demo and it looks to tick all the right boxes so far.

VST stands for virtual studio technology. VST's are "an audio plug-in software interface that integrates software synthesizer and effects in digital audio workstations", like a "reverb" to add echos or a piano plugin that emulates the sounds of a piano. Some popular VST's everyone raves about are Serum, Kontakt and Strobe 2. I actually have no idea how these wire up to either the DAW or the midi controller.

OK, so whats a synth? How is a synth different to a VST? A VST is usually placed into the category of an effect or an instrument. A synth is a sound synthesizer, used to create sounds. A synth can be hardware or software and it's always an instrument. A VST is always software and it can be an instrument, effect etc. This is a great time to stop looking around for "stuff" and confusing myself with terminology.

I know Music has lots of rules, often broken in awesome ways; but I won't break any of them knowingly, without first understanding the rules themselves.

Music theory will help me make more deliberate decisions when composing song concepts. Less guesswork and frustration, means more creating. If I can listen to the music I like and know why I like it, why it's doing what it's doing, I can use deliberate practice to bring that into my own arrangements. Win win.
