Tonality & Atonality

A "tonal center" refers to a pattern of notes which circle around a central note.
The "Tonic" is:

  • the goal note of a melody or key
  • often the first and usually the last note of a melody
  • the first note of the scale from which a tune is created

Atonal music has no clear tonic pitch.

Note: The tonic pitch doesn't need to sound to be present.

Scales
A song that has a single tonal centre, or tonic, is in a single "key". Usually, these notes can be ordered as a series of steps, with the tonic pitch as the first and last note.

Key contepts

  • Harmony: Notes and chords
  • Harmonic function: Describes the role a chord plays in the creation of the harmony
  • Tonic:The first note in any scale
  • Predominant: Any chord which resolves to a dominant chord
  • Dominant: A chord which takes us back to the tonic?
  • Harmonic hierarchy: Some chords are more important than others in a composition. Hierarchy is a tree of importance.

QUIZ: Tonality, Harmony, Acoustics

Q: What is the function of a tonic harmony?
A: Stable, the harmony that a song usually starts and ends with.

Q: What is the function of a dominant harmony?
A: Unstable, the harmony that demands resolution
